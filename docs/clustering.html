Clustering in Temporal Networks

This example demonstrates pathpy's features to detect and visualise temporal-topological cluster patterns in time series data. For this, let us consider the following (synthetic) temporal network, which shows no obvious cluster structure, neither in terms of temporal activations nor in terms of the topology of interactions.

To highlight the fact that the topology of interactions does not exhibit any structure, we can visualise the time-aggregated network corresponding to the temporal interaction sequence above. The aggregate topology of this system turns out to be a random graph.

pathpy allows us to simulate (and visualise) dynamical processes in temporal networks. The following animation shows the sequene of visited nodes for a random walk process in the temporal network above. A closer inspections of the sequence of visited nodes reveals that certain groups of nodes are visited in sequence more often than we would expect, considering the random topology of the network. The reason for these patterns are complex higher-order dependencies in the sequence of time-stamped interactions. These higher-order dependencies are not captured by the network topology. They are due to temporal correlations that interact with the network topology in a non-trivial way, generating temporal-topological clusters that can neither be detected by standard clustering algorithms.

The framework of higher- and multi-order network models that is at the core of pathpy's approach to network analytics captures higher-order dependencies in time series data.
